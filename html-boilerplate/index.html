Mandrills are the largest monkeys in the world and are native to west central Africa. They are known for their red and blue faces, long canine teeth, and furry heads. Mandrills are omnivores that eat fruits, seeds, roots, insects, reptiles, and amphibians. They live in tropical rainforests, forested savannahs, and montane forests up to 6,571 ft. (2,000 meters).
